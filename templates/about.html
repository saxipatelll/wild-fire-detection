About

Project documentation — objective, methodology, results and future scope.

01 — Vision
Our Vision
FireFound envisions a world where technology serves as the first line of defense against wildfire disasters. We believe that early, accurate detection can save lives, protect ecosystems, and preserve natural resources for future generations. Our mission is to make advanced wildfire detection accessible, reliable, and deployable anywhere it's needed most.
02 — Problem Statement
The Problem
Wildfires are among the most destructive natural disasters. Early automated detection from images — satellite, drone, or camera feeds — can provide critical advance warning. This project demonstrates that a carefully engineered classical pipeline can achieve competitive accuracy without deep learning, making it deployable on resource-constrained hardware.
03 — Methodology
How It Works
The pipeline processes each image through a multi-stage feature extraction and classification workflow:
  • Images are resized to 128×128 px and converted to grayscale and HSV color spaces.
  • HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients) descriptors capture gradient direction and magnitude — encoding the chaotic edge patterns of fire and smoke (~3780 dims).
  • HSV channel histograms (32 bins each) model fire's distinctive color distribution in hue-saturation-value space (~96 dims).
  • A 4×4 spatial grid of grayscale statistics (mean, std, skewness proxy) captures local texture variation (~48 dims).
  • All features are concatenated into a ~3924-dim vector, scaled with StandardScaler, then compressed via PCA (95% variance retained, ~300 dims).
  • An SVM with RBF kernel (C and γ tuned via 5-fold GridSearchCV) classifies the PCA features into fire / no-fire.

05 — Future Scope
Next Steps
Several enhancements can push accuracy toward 95%+ without deep learning:
Data Augmentation Horizontal flips, brightness jitter, and random crops to expand training diversity.
Ensemble SVM Bagging multiple SVM models trained on feature subsets — reduces variance.
LBP Features Local Binary Patterns add rotation-invariant texture representation.
Colour Moments Statistical moments (mean, std, skew) per channel in RGB + LAB spaces.
Hard Negative Mining Iteratively retrain on misclassified no-fire images that look like fire.
Real-Time Integration Connect to live drone/satellite feeds with sliding-window detection.
